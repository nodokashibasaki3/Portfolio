Nodoka Shibasaki

I'm a third-year Electrical and Computer Engineering student at Northeastern University studying robotics and control. I'm currently interning at OMRON SINIC X in Tokyo working on soft robotics learning and control. During my undergrad, I've conducted research at Silicon Synapses Lab with Professor Ramezani Alireza and at Shepherd's Lab with Professor Max Shepherd. My research is motivated by the goal of enabling humans to seamlessly and safely work together with robots in their daily lives.

My early work in athletic training and at Stanford Sports Medicine Lab initially drew me toward biomechanics and pre-med. But as I began working on rehabilitation robotics in college, I found myself more captivated by the engineering challenge of creating adaptive systems that work with human capabilities. I'm interested in developing safe, adaptive and replicable robotic systems that enable intuitive human-robot collaboration.

Research Projects

Multi-Modal Locomotion Robot

Jan 2025 ‚Äì July 2025

Sim-to-real transfer remains a major bottleneck for deploying advanced control policies on multi-modal robots in the wild. The challenge persists because real-world environments introduce unpredictable dynamics and hardware constraints that are hard to model or anticipate in simulation. For the M4 platform, I built a reproducible pipeline that bridges simulation and field deployment, integrating ROS 2, ArduPilot, and Dockerized tools for energy-aware planning and robust control. This system reduced deployment time by 40% and improved energy efficiency by 18% in field trials, enabling rapid iteration and reliable real-world testing for our research group.

Ankle Exoskeleton

Sep 2024 ‚Äì Jun 2025

Modular, adaptable exoskeletons are key to advancing assistive robotics for rehabilitation and performance. However, designing hardware that is both customizable and robust for diverse users remains an open problem due to the complexity of human biomechanics and the need for rapid iteration. I designed a modular ankle exoskeleton platform, developing a flexible CAD system and integrating sensor data pipelines for rapid prototyping and testing. This enabled our team to iterate on hardware and control strategies efficiently, laying the groundwork for future machine learning-driven gait assistance studies.
