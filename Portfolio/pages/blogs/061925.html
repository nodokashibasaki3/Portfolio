Robotics Lab Experience

June 19, 2025

Before joining the lab, I didn't know what a robot was. I barely knew anything.

I had some experience with machine learning and motion capture software, which helped me get the research assistant position. I reached out to Max, the PI of Shepherd Lab, because the lab focused on biomechanics and robotics, which matched my interests.

My first project involved power wiring for a hip exoskeleton. I had only done basic Arduino circuits and didn't know how to use fuses, Dupont connectors, soldering tools, or crimpers. I learned everything from scratch and built a working circuit.

In the second project, I debugged a machine learning algorithm using the teacher-student method. The loss was high, but I improved it by implementing LOSO and using clean data from the Qualisys motion capture system. Accuracy rose to 98 percent, with a 200 percent performance improvement.

For the third project, I designed a CAD backplate for the ankle exoskeleton. The original prototype had flaws and required tape to hold wires. I created a modular backplate with a hip curve and clean wire routing.

Through these projects, I gained skills in electronics, software, and mechanical design. I also learned from researchers with backgrounds in biology and engineering. Everyone was kind and always greeted me on campus.

This lab made me love robotics. I'm grateful for the experience and wish them all the best.
